Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this course, students will be able to:

  1. Discuss classic literature and historical figures in evolutionary biology
  2. Explain the molecular and genetic basis for evolution
  3. Explain mechanisms of evolution (natural selection, migration, genetic drift, mutation, nonrandom mating) and their relationship to population genetics
  4. Apply Hardy-Weinberg calculations to various populations
  5. Describe mechanisms of speciation and origin of new species
  6. Evaluate how the fossil record relates to current views of evolution
  7. Analyze evolutionary trends demonstrated by phylogeny
  8. Investigate the course of evolution in prokaryotes and eukaryotes, with emphasis on hominin evolution
  9. Compose disciplinary-appropriate texts: scientific essays, posters, manuscripts, and mock grant proposals
